Manual Lymphatic Drainage

MLD & restorative wellness.

Manual Lymphatic Drainage is a light-pressure, precise technique that encourages the natural movement of lymph fluid — the body's system for clearing fluid and waste and supporting immune function. The result is a deeply restorative experience that helps you feel lighter, clearer, and more balanced.

  • Supports reduction of swelling and fluid retention.
  • Encourages circulation and the body's natural healing response.
  • Stimulates the parasympathetic nervous system, leading to relaxation and reduced stress.
  • Improved lymphatic flow contributes to better skin health and enhanced skin renewal.
  • Applied with a Registered Nurse's awareness of anatomy and contraindications.

Nervous System Calming

A path back to rest & repair.

The slow, rhythmic quality of lymphatic work does more than move fluid — it signals safety to the nervous system. Many clients experience MLD as profoundly calming, helping the body shift out of a stress-driven "fight or flight" state and into the "rest and digest" mode where genuine recovery happens.

For those carrying tension, fatigue, or the aftermath of a demanding season of life, these sessions offer a quiet, restorative reset — clinically informed and entirely unhurried.

Pre & Post-Op Recovery

Supporting surgical recovery, every stage.

Surgery places real demands on the body. Guided by a Registered Nurse, MLD supports your body's own systems before and after a procedure — easing swelling and working alongside your surgeon's protocol.

  1. Pre-Op · Preparation

    Optimizing your baseline

    Pre-operative MLD can help reduce existing fluid retention and prepare the lymphatic system for a cleaner starting point.

  2. Post-Op · Early Phase

    Gentle decongestion

    Once cleared by your surgeon, light MLD encourages the movement of post-surgical fluid — easing swelling and pain, reducing bruising and tightness, and facilitating faster healing. It can also help decrease infection risk by aiding the clearance of bacteria and viruses to healthy lymph nodes.

  3. Post-Op · Active Healing

    Supporting the timeline

    Regular sessions support circulation and comfort as tissues heal, diminishing fibrosis and thick scarring, with technique adjusted to your recovery markers.

Pre & Post Aesthetic Surgery

Recovery care for aesthetic procedures.

Aesthetic and cosmetic procedures deserve recovery care that protects the result. Post-operative MLD is a well-established part of many aesthetic recovery plans — helping manage swelling and fluid, easing the feeling of tightness, and supporting a smooth, settled outcome.

Care is always coordinated with your surgeon's timing and clearance, and delivered with the clinical awareness that aesthetic recovery requires.

Pregnancy, Post C-Section & Breastfeeding

Gentle support through pregnancy & postpartum.

MLD is generally considered safe during the second and third trimesters of pregnancy, and can be beneficial in managing the fluid retention and swelling that commonly occur during this period.

  • Helps with relaxation and stress reduction.
  • Promotes better circulation for enhanced milk flow while breastfeeding.
  • Assists with engorgement.

For expectant mothers, I employ modified positioning and refrain from directly working over the abdomen. Please note that I do not perform MLD during the first trimester.
