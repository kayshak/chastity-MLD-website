The Clinical Rationale

Why pre- & post-operative MLD matters.

Manual Lymphatic Drainage is a long-established part of many pre- and post-surgical recovery plans. Pre-surgical MLD reduces interstitial fluid, facilitating better tissue perfusion and decreasing tissue stretch, while post-operative MLD facilitates tissue healing by eliminating accumulated fluids, proteins, and cellular debris from the surgical site. Performed by a trained RN, it supports the body's own systems through the most demanding phase of healing.

Manages edema

Gently encourages the clearance of post-surgical fluid that drives swelling, helping patients feel and look more comfortable, sooner.

Supports tissue healing, decreases infection risk

By supporting circulation and lymphatic flow, MLD helps create the conditions tissues need to recover and settle.

Eases fibrosis & tightness

Consistent, technique-driven support can help soften the feeling of firmness and tightness as patients progress.

Improves comfort & mobility

Reduced swelling and tension often translate into greater comfort and easier movement during early recovery.

Reassures anxious patients

A knowledgeable, calming presence during recovery reduces anxiety and reinforces confidence in your care.

Reinforces your protocol

Every session echoes your post-op instructions, helping patients stay compliant and engaged in their recovery.

An honest note: MLD is a supportive therapy, not a guarantee of any specific surgical result. Outcomes depend on the procedure, the patient, and your surgical plan — care is always delivered as a complement to it.

For Referring Surgeons

Frequently asked questions.

When can patients begin post-operative MLD?

Timing follows your clearance and protocol. Many surgeons recommend starting within the first days to weeks after surgery; I always defer to your specific instructions for each patient and procedure.

How many sessions do patients typically need?

It varies by procedure, the degree of swelling, and individual healing. Plans are tailored per patient and revisited as recovery progresses, rather than sold as a fixed prescription.

Will you follow my post-operative protocol?

Always. Your protocol leads. Treatment timing, technique, and progression are guided by your instructions, and I work strictly within RN scope as a complement to your care.

How will you communicate with my practice?

Clearly and discreetly. I provide professional updates on patient progress and promptly flag anything — such as a possible complication — that warrants your attention.

What training do you have in lymphatic drainage?

I'm a Registered Nurse with specialized training in the Vodder Method of Manual Lymphatic Drainage — the recognized gold-standard technique — alongside a background in patient education and chronic disease management.

Do you offer in-home visits?

Yes, concierge in-home recovery visits are available when appropriate, supporting comfort and continuity of care for patients who would benefit from them.
