Pre & Post-Op Recovery.

How Manual Lymphatic Drainage supports the body before and after surgery — an educational look at the recovery timeline and its physical benefits.

Why it matters

Recovery is a process — not a moment.

Surgery places real demands on the body. Swelling, fluid retention, and inflammation are a natural part of healing — but how they're managed can influence comfort and the overall trajectory of recovery. Manual Lymphatic Drainage, guided by a Registered Nurse, supports the body's own systems through each stage.

The Timeline

A stage-by-stage view of lymphatic recovery.

Before surgery — preparation

  1. Pre-Op · Weeks Before

    Optimizing your baseline

    Pre-operative MLD can help reduce existing fluid retention and prepare the lymphatic system, supporting a cleaner starting point for healing.

  2. Pre-Op · Final Days

    Education & planning

    Understanding what to expect — positioning, movement, and how lymphatic care will fit alongside your surgeon's instructions.

After surgery — restoration

  1. Post-Op · Early Phase

    Gentle decongestion

    Once cleared by your surgeon, light MLD encourages the movement of post-surgical fluid, helping ease swelling and the sensation of tightness.

  2. Post-Op · Active Healing

    Supporting the timeline

    Regular sessions support circulation and comfort as tissues heal, with technique adjusted to your recovery markers at each visit.

  3. Post-Op · Long-Term

    Refinement & wellbeing

    Continued care supports a smooth, settled result and a return to everyday wellness.

Physical Benefits

What MLD can support around surgery.

Less swelling

Encourages the clearance of post-surgical fluid that contributes to swelling and discomfort.

Comfort & mobility

May ease the feeling of tightness and heaviness, supporting a more comfortable recovery.

An optimized timeline

Works alongside your surgeon's protocol to support the body's natural healing response.
