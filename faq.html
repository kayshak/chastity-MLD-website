The Basics

Understanding MLD & the lymphatic system.

What is the lymphatic system?

The lymphatic system is a crucial component of your body's circulatory and immune systems. It is a network of vessels and lymph nodes that collect fluid, proteins, and cellular debris from between your cells and transport it back toward your heart. Your lymph nodes filter this fluid and serve as immune-system checkpoints, combating bacteria and viruses. This system maintains fluid balance throughout your body. An active, healthy lymphatic system fights infections more effectively and maintains healthy fluid balance throughout your tissues.

What is Manual Lymphatic Drainage (MLD)?

Manual Lymphatic Drainage (MLD) is a specialized medical technique developed by researchers and physicians specializing in the lymphatic system. This manual technique mimics the body's natural fluid-movement process, albeit at an accelerated rate, thereby aiding the recovery of a sluggish or compromised lymphatic system and alleviating swelling. MLD is based on established protocols and is recognized by medical professionals as a safe and effective treatment for multiple conditions.

Is MLD the same as "lymphatic massage" or "body sculpting"?

Technically, no — Manual Lymphatic Drainage (MLD) is not the same as the "lymphatic massage" or "body sculpting" services often advertised. While these services may use similar terminology, they differ significantly in technique, purpose, and outcomes. Understanding these distinctions enables you to select the appropriate treatment for your needs and set realistic expectations. Generally, these sculpting sessions are not suitable for medical conditions such as post-surgical recovery or lymphedema due to their vigorous nature.

Why the distinction matters: For clients who are post-surgical, have lymphedema, complex medical histories, chronic inflammation, autoimmune conditions, injury recovery, are pregnant, or are seeking therapeutic wellness, using the incorrect technique can be counterproductive at best and potentially hazardous at worst. Firm pressure, aggressive manipulation, or improper drainage techniques can:

  • Damage compromised or healing tissues
  • Increase swelling rather than reduce it
  • Cause complications such as seromas or hematomas
  • Delay recovery or potentially harm surgical results

I provide authentic MLD using established medical protocols and evidence-based techniques developed for therapeutic purposes.

Safety & Suitability

Is MLD right for you?

Is MLD safe for me? Are there risks?

Manual Lymphatic Drainage (MLD) is generally considered safe and is one of the gentlest forms of therapeutic bodywork. However, there are certain conditions where MLD should be avoided or modified.

If you have recently undergone surgery and still have bandages, sutures, drains, or other surgical interventions, please rest assured that the post-operative treatment I provide is extremely gentle and adheres to clinical best practices. My protocol includes:

  • Refraining from touching open incisions
  • Refraining from removing dressings over incisions
  • Wearing gloves when working close to open incisions
  • Employing the gentlest touch to guide lymphatic fluid away from surgical sites

During your initial consultation, we will review your health history to determine whether MLD is appropriate for your specific situation. If you have any medical conditions, we will discuss how to safely adapt the treatment to your needs. When in doubt, please consult your physician — I am always happy to communicate with your healthcare team to ensure coordinated and safe care.

Who should NOT receive MLD?
  • Active infection or fever — MLD could spread infection through your system.
  • Congestive heart failure — increased fluid movement can strain the heart.
  • Blood clots (DVT) — risk of clot movement.
  • Acute kidney problems — your kidneys may not be able to process additional fluid.
  • Active or untreated cancer — discuss with your oncologist first. Many cancer survivors receive MLD safely, but timing matters.
What if I'm pregnant?

MLD is generally considered safe during the second and third trimesters of pregnancy and can be beneficial in managing the fluid retention and swelling that commonly occur during this period. For expectant mothers, I employ modified positioning and refrain from directly working over the abdomen. Please note that I do not perform MLD during the first trimester.

Your Sessions

Before, during & after your visit.

How many sessions will I require?

The number of sessions you'll need depends on your specific condition, goals, and how your body responds to treatment. Every individual heals differently, so I will collaborate with you to develop a personalized treatment plan. During your initial visit, we'll create a plan based on your personal factors. Most clients benefit from MLD for up to six months post-operatively, with gradually decreasing frequency, to address any recurring swelling and manage scar tissue.

How do I prepare for my session?

Hydration:

  • Consume ample water in the hours preceding your appointment.
  • Refrain from alcohol for 24 hours prior to treatment.

What to wear:

  • Most individuals undress completely or to their underwear for sessions. You will remain covered during treatment, with only the body part being worked on exposed.
  • It's generally beneficial to wear loose-fitting clothing after your sessions, but any attire is acceptable.
  • Compression garments will be removed for the session, and I will assist with reapplication afterward if required.

Medications:

  • Continue all prescribed medications as usual.
  • Inform me if you are taking blood thinners (MLD is gentle, but it's important to note).
  • Inform me if you take injections or wear any type of medicated patches.
What should I do after my session?
  • Consume ample water throughout the day. This facilitates the elimination of lymph fluid mobilized during treatment.
  • Refrain from alcohol for 24 hours following treatment.
  • Allow yourself some rest for a few hours after your session.
  • Gentle activities such as walking, swimming, or yoga can aid lymph flow.
  • Avoid strenuous exercise for 12–24 hours.
  • Avoid hot tubs for 12–24 hours.
  • Pay attention to your body's signals.
What should I expect after my MLD session?

Some potential experiences include:

  • Increased urination, which is a positive indicator of fluid elimination.
  • A sense of relaxation and mild fatigue, which is normal.
  • Mild fatigue to low flu-like symptoms as your body processes and eliminates waste; this typically subsides within 24 hours.
  • Temporary increased thirst; drink water as needed.
  • Improved mobility and a lighter sensation due to reduced swelling and stiffness.
When should I contact you?
  • Unexpected pain or discomfort.
  • Signs of infection (redness, heat, fever).
  • Unusual increase in swelling.
  • Any concerns regarding your treatment response.

For post-surgical clients, it is imperative to also report any concerning symptoms to your surgeon.
